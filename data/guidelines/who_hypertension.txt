WHO/ISH GUIDELINE: HYPERTENSION MANAGEMENT IN PRIMARY CARE
Source: WHO/ISH 2023 Hypertension Guidelines & WHO HEARTS Technical Package

ICD-10 CODES:
- I10   — Essential (primary) hypertension
- I11.9 — Hypertensive heart disease without heart failure
- I13.10 — Hypertensive heart and chronic kidney disease
- I16.0 — Hypertensive urgency
- I16.1 — Hypertensive emergency

DEFINITION:
Hypertension: sustained systolic BP ≥140 mmHg and/or diastolic BP ≥90 mmHg
on ≥2 separate readings on ≥2 occasions.

STAGING (2023):
  Normal:          SBP <120 and DBP <80
  Elevated (prehypertension): SBP 120-129 and DBP <80
  Stage 1 HTN:     SBP 130-139 or DBP 80-89
  Stage 2 HTN:     SBP ≥140 or DBP ≥90
  Hypertensive Urgency:  SBP ≥180 and/or DBP ≥120, no target organ damage
  Hypertensive Emergency: SBP ≥180 and/or DBP ≥120, WITH target organ damage

TARGET ORGAN DAMAGE — look for:
- Cardiac: LVH, heart failure, angina, MI
- Cerebral: stroke, TIA, cognitive impairment
- Renal: CKD, proteinuria
- Eyes: retinopathy
- Peripheral vascular disease

CARDIOVASCULAR RISK STRATIFICATION:
Low risk:    Stage 1 HTN, no additional risk factors
Medium risk: Stage 1-2 HTN, 1-2 risk factors
High risk:   Stage 2 HTN, ≥3 risk factors, or target organ damage, or diabetes, or CKD

TREATMENT INITIATION:
- Lifestyle modification for ALL patients
- Drug therapy: initiated at Stage 1 if high CV risk, or at Stage 2 for all

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS (ABCDE):
  A — Alcohol restriction (<2 units/day)
  B — Blood pressure monitoring at home
  C — Cessation of smoking
  D — Diet: DASH diet, restrict salt to <5g/day, increase fruit/vegetables
  E — Exercise: ≥150 min moderate aerobic activity/week, weight reduction

FIRST-LINE MEDICATIONS (WHO HEARTS simplified protocol):
STEP 1 (Monotherapy):
  - Amlodipine 5mg OD (CCB) — preferred in elderly, Afro-Caribbean, isolated systolic
  - OR Lisinopril 5-10mg OD (ACEi) — preferred in diabetics, proteinuria, heart failure
  - OR Losartan 50mg OD (ARB) — if ACEi not tolerated (cough)
  - OR Hydrochlorothiazide 12.5-25mg OD (thiazide) — add-on preferred

STEP 2 (Dual therapy — if BP not controlled on monotherapy):
  - Amlodipine + ACEi/ARB (preferred combination)
  - OR ACEi/ARB + Thiazide
  AVOID: ACEi + ARB combination (increased renal risk)

STEP 3 (Triple therapy):
  - Amlodipine + ACEi/ARB + Thiazide

STEP 4 (Resistant hypertension — refer if possible):
  - Add Spironolactone 25-50mg OD
  - OR Bisoprolol 5-10mg OD (especially if heart failure/tachycardia)
  - OR Alpha-blocker (Doxazosin 4mg OD)

SPECIAL POPULATIONS:
Diabetes: Target BP <130/80. Prefer ACEi or ARB (renoprotective).
CKD: Target <130/80. ACEi/ARB preferred. Avoid thiazide if eGFR <30.
Elderly (≥65y): Target 130-139/70-79. Start low, go slow. Avoid postural hypotension.
Pregnancy: Use Methyldopa, Nifedipine, Labetalol. AVOID ACEi, ARB, thiazide.
Heart failure: ACEi/ARB + low-dose beta-blocker. Avoid CCB (neg. inotropy).

HYPERTENSIVE URGENCY (no target organ damage):
- Reduce BP over 24-48 hours (rapid reduction is dangerous)
- Oral Amlodipine 5mg, or oral Nifedipine modified-release 30mg
- Increase existing antihypertensive
- Review at 48-72 hours

HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCY (with target organ damage):
- REFER IMMEDIATELY to higher-level facility
- IV Labetalol (if available) or oral Nifedipine SR
- Do NOT use Nifedipine capsules sublingually (rapid drop = stroke risk)
- IV Hydralazine for hypertensive emergency in pregnancy

MONITORING:
- Monthly until BP <140/90, then every 3-6 months
- Annual: electrolytes, creatinine, fasting glucose, urine dipstick
- Adherence assessment at every visit (pill count, BP diary)

MEDICATION ADHERENCE — rural context:
- Once-daily dosing improves adherence
- Involve family members in BP monitoring
- Fixed-dose combinations preferred (fewer pills)
- Community health worker reinforcement visits recommended

PATIENT EDUCATION:
- Hypertension is a lifelong condition; medications usually life-long
- Do not stop medications when BP "feels normal"
- Home BP monitoring: sit quietly 5 min, 2 readings 1-2 min apart, morning and evening
- Signs of emergency: severe headache, blurred vision, chest pain, focal neurological symptoms → seek care immediately
