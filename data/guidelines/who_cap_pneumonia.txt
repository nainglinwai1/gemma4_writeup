WHO GUIDELINE: COMMUNITY-ACQUIRED PNEUMONIA (CAP) — SIMPLIFIED RURAL CLINIC VERSION
Source: WHO Integrated Management of Adolescent and Adult Illness (IMAI), 2024 update

ICD-10 CODES:
- J18.9 — Pneumonia, unspecified organism
- J15.9 — Unspecified bacterial pneumonia
- J18.1 — Lobar pneumonia, unspecified organism

DEFINITION:
Community-acquired pneumonia (CAP) is an acute infection of the pulmonary parenchyma
acquired outside of a hospital setting. It is one of the leading causes of morbidity
and mortality in low-resource settings.

CLINICAL FEATURES:
One or more of:
- Cough (productive or dry)
- Fever (≥38°C) or hypothermia (<35.5°C in severe cases)
- Pleuritic chest pain
- Dyspnea or tachypnea (RR ≥20/min)

Physical signs: dullness to percussion, bronchial breath sounds, crackles, reduced air entry.

SEVERITY ASSESSMENT — CRB-65 Score (for use in primary care settings without labs):
Score 1 point each for:
  C — Confusion (new)
  R — Respiratory rate ≥30/min
  B — Systolic BP <90 mmHg OR diastolic BP ≤60 mmHg
  65 — Age ≥65 years

CRB-65 Score Interpretation:
  0   → Low risk. Consider home treatment.
  1-2 → Moderate risk. Hospital admission recommended.
  3-4 → High risk. Urgent hospital admission. Consider ICU.

SpO2 <92% on room air = marker of severity; consider referral.

TREATMENT — Outpatient (mild CAP, CRB-65 = 0):
FIRST LINE (non-penicillin-allergic):
  Amoxicillin 500mg PO TDS × 5 days
  OR Amoxicillin-Clavulanate 625mg PO TDS × 5 days (if aspiration suspected)

PENICILLIN ALLERGY:
  Azithromycin 500mg PO OD × 5 days
  OR Doxycycline 100mg PO BD × 7 days (avoid in pregnancy)
  OR Levofloxacin 750mg PO OD × 5 days (if available)

Atypical coverage (if Mycoplasma/Chlamydia suspected):
  Add Azithromycin 500mg OD × 5 days to beta-lactam

TREATMENT — Inpatient (moderate-severe CAP, CRB-65 ≥1):
  IV Benzylpenicillin 1.2g QDS + IV/PO Azithromycin 500mg OD
  OR IV Co-amoxiclav 1.2g TDS + IV/PO Azithromycin 500mg OD (if penicillin-allergic: Levofloxacin 750mg IV/PO OD)
  Duration: 5-7 days (switch to oral when clinically improving)

SUPPORTIVE CARE:
- Supplemental oxygen if SpO2 <94% (target 94-98%)
- IV fluids if dehydrated or unable to take oral
- Antipyretics (Paracetamol 1g QDS PRN)
- Encourage fluid intake

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS IN RURAL/RESOURCE-LIMITED SETTINGS:
- If chest X-ray unavailable, clinical diagnosis is acceptable
- TB must be excluded in endemic regions (productive cough >2 weeks, night sweats, weight loss)
- HIV-positive patients: consider PCP (Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia) if CD4 <200; treat with Co-trimoxazole 960mg TDS × 21 days
- Refer if: SpO2 <90%, unable to take oral medications, CRB-65 ≥2, clinical deterioration on therapy

FOLLOW-UP:
- Reassess in 48-72 hours if managed as outpatient
- Clinical improvement expected within 3 days
- Persistent fever or worsening → re-evaluate, consider TB, resistant organism, empyema

PATIENT EDUCATION:
- Complete the full antibiotic course even if feeling better
- Rest and increase fluid intake
- Return immediately if breathing worsens, confusion develops, or unable to eat/drink
- Smoking cessation counseling if applicable
- Pneumococcal and influenza vaccination for eligible patients (elderly, immunocompromised)
