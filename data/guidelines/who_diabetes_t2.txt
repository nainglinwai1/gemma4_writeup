WHO GUIDELINE: TYPE 2 DIABETES MELLITUS — PRIMARY CARE MANAGEMENT
Source: WHO 2022 Global Diabetes Compact & IDF Clinical Practice Recommendations 2023

ICD-10 CODES:
- E11.9  — Type 2 diabetes mellitus without complications
- E11.65 — Type 2 diabetes mellitus with hyperglycemia
- E11.649 — Type 2 DM with hypoglycemia
- E11.40 — Type 2 DM with diabetic neuropathy, unspecified
- E11.319 — Type 2 DM with unspecified diabetic retinopathy
- E11.65 — Type 2 DM with hyperglycemia (DKA: E11.10)

DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA (any one of):
  1. Fasting plasma glucose ≥7.0 mmol/L (126 mg/dL) — confirmed on 2 occasions
  2. 2h plasma glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L during 75g OGTT
  3. HbA1c ≥48 mmol/mol (≥6.5%)
  4. Random plasma glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L with symptoms of hyperglycemia

If results borderline or equivocal, repeat test on a different day.

GLYCEMIC TARGETS:
  HbA1c < 53 mmol/mol (<7.0%) — general target
  HbA1c < 48 mmol/mol (<6.5%) — if achievable without hypoglycemia (younger, early diagnosis)
  HbA1c < 64 mmol/mol (<8.0%) — frail elderly, limited life expectancy, hypoglycemia-prone

Fasting glucose: 4.0-7.0 mmol/L
Post-prandial (2h):  <10.0 mmol/L

LIFESTYLE INTERVENTIONS (first-line for all):
- Weight loss 5-10% if overweight: significant glycemic benefit
- Physical activity: 150 min/week moderate intensity
- Medical nutrition therapy: reduce refined carbs, increase fibre, limit saturated fat
- Self-monitoring of blood glucose (SMBG) if available

PHARMACOTHERAPY — Step-wise approach:

STEP 1 — Monotherapy:
  FIRST LINE: Metformin 500mg OD with food, titrate to 500-1000mg BD/TDS over 4-8 weeks
  (MAX: 2000mg/day; avoid if eGFR <30)
  
  ALTERNATIVES if Metformin contraindicated/not tolerated:
  - SGLT2 inhibitor (Empagliflozin 10mg OD) — cardiorenal benefits, weight neutral
  - Sulfonylurea (Glibenclamide 2.5-5mg OD-BD or Gliclazide MR 30mg OD)
    ⚠ Risk of hypoglycemia — use with caution in elderly

STEP 2 — Dual therapy (HbA1c not at target after 3 months):
  Metformin + one of:
  - SGLT2 inhibitor (preferred if CV/renal disease)
  - GLP-1 agonist (if available: Liraglutide 0.6-1.2mg SC OD — weight loss benefit)
  - Sulfonylurea (cost-effective, widely available)
  - DPP-4 inhibitor (Sitagliptin 100mg OD) — well tolerated, low hypoglycemia risk

STEP 3 — Triple therapy:
  Metformin + SGLT2i + Sulfonylurea, or
  Metformin + two oral agents as above

STEP 4 — Insulin:
  Basal insulin (Insulin Glargine 10 units SC at bedtime, titrate by 2 units every 3 days)
  OR Intermediate insulin (NPH Insulin BD if Glargine unavailable)
  Continue Metformin, consider stopping sulfonylurea when insulin started

HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT:
  Mild (conscious): 15g fast-acting carbohydrate (glucose tabs, sugary drink, 3-4 biscuits)
  Repeat in 15 min if not improved; follow with complex carb snack
  Severe (unconscious): IV Dextrose 50% 25mL; or Glucagon 1mg IM if available
  Identify cause: missed meal, excess dose, unusual activity, alcohol

CARDIOVASCULAR RISK REDUCTION:
  - Aspirin 75mg OD (if established CVD or high CV risk, >40yo)
  - Statin (Atorvastatin 20-40mg OD) — all T2DM >40yo with CV risk factors
  - ACEi/ARB if hypertensive or proteinuric
  - BP target <130/80 mmHg

DIABETES COMPLICATIONS — ANNUAL SCREENING:
  Kidneys: urine albumin:creatinine ratio (ACR), serum creatinine/eGFR
  Eyes: fundoscopy (refer to ophthalmology if retinopathy)
  Feet: monofilament test, pedal pulses, visual inspection (every visit)
  Neuropathy: 10g monofilament, ankle reflexes, vibration sense
  Cardiovascular: ECG, lipid profile

DIABETIC FOOT CARE (rural setting):
  - Daily foot inspection
  - Proper footwear — no bare feet
  - Prompt wound care: refer ulcers to wound care specialist
  - ANY foot ulcer = urgent review; infected ulcer = IV antibiotics, referral

SPECIAL POPULATIONS — RURAL CONTEXT:
  If HbA1c unavailable: use fasting glucose to monitor
  If SMBG strips limited: prioritize fasting and pre-meal monitoring
  Metformin and Glibenclamide on WHO Essential Medicines List — widely available
  Insulin storage: standard refrigeration 2-8°C; room temp ≤25°C for 28 days (open vial)

SICK DAY RULES:
  - Do NOT stop medications during illness (except Metformin if vomiting/dehydrated)
  - Monitor glucose more frequently
  - Maintain fluid intake
  - Seek care if: glucose >16 mmol/L, severe vomiting, unable to eat for >24h

PATIENT EDUCATION:
  - Understand the link between food, activity, and glucose
  - Recognize hypoglycemia symptoms: shakiness, sweating, confusion, palpitations
  - Always carry fast-acting glucose
  - Importance of foot care and not going barefoot
  - Medication adherence: never stop without talking to health worker
